# TCF Resource Strategy

**Date** : 2026-03-24  
**Goal** : Use effort where it gives the highest return for `TCF` performance, especially `QCM`

---

## Core Idea

The work should happen in this order:

1. **Get back on your feet**
   Rebuild grammar and vocabulary with stable books.
2. **Translate that foundation into TCF tasks**
   Use targeted `QCM` drills for structure de langue, vocabulaire, CE, and CO.
3. **Train the exam itself**
   Use sample tests, timing, correction patterns, and repeated weak-area drills.

This means we should **not** jump straight into endless mock tests.
If the base is shaky, mock tests mostly measure confusion.
If the base is rebuilt first, mock tests become much more useful.

## Recommended Study Phases

## Phase A: Rebuild

**Main ratio** :
- `45%` grammar spine
- `30%` vocabulary spine
- `25%` small TCF drill sets

Primary materials:
- `Grammaire Essentielle du Francais B2`
- `Vocabulaire Progressif du Francais`
- `Structure de langue` series in small chunks

Goal:
- rebuild automaticity
- stop obvious grammar mistakes
- recover core vocab and test confidence

Exit signal:
- you regularly score well on short structure drills
- recurring errors become narrower and more predictable

## Phase B: Targeted TCF Acceleration

**Main ratio** :
- `25%` grammar/vocab review
- `50%` TCF drill bank
- `25%` section-based timed work

Primary materials:
- `Structure de langue` series
- `Expressions et Vocabulaire 1`
- `CE 3`, `CE 5`, `Entrainement 5`, `Entrainement 6`
- selected sections from `ABC TCF`, `250 activites`, `Reussir le TCF`

Goal:
- turn knowledge into exam reflexes
- improve distractor elimination
- identify weak categories precisely

Exit signal:
- weak areas are clearly named
- timing pressure no longer breaks accuracy

## Phase C: Simulation And Repair

**Main ratio** :
- `15%` foundation review
- `35%` weak-point repair
- `50%` mock tests and timed sections

Primary materials:
- `ABC TCF`
- `250 activites`
- `Reussir le TCF`
- `TCF TV5 NEW`
- audio banks for `CO`

Goal:
- simulate the test
- manage time
- stabilize performance under exam conditions

Rule:
- every mock test must feed back into grammar, vocab, and error tracking

## Best Use Of Effort

### What To Do A Lot
- short daily grammar review
- short daily vocabulary review
- `QCM` structure de langue
- correction of repeated mistakes
- timed mini sets
- recycling wrong answers until they become easy

### What To Limit
- random browsing across too many books
- long passive reading without conversion into questions
- full mocks too early
- studying rare expressions before fixing core errors

### What To Avoid
- treating every resource as equal
- spending most of the time on speaking here
- collecting vocabulary without repeated retrieval

---

## Recommended Session Template

For a normal `60-90 min` session:

1. `15-20 min` grammar foundation
   One lesson or one focused point from `Grammaire Essentielle`.
2. `10-15 min` vocabulary foundation
   One theme or one review set from `Vocabulaire Progressif`.
3. `20-30 min` TCF drill
   Structure de langue or vocab `QCM`.
4. `15-20 min` correction and logging
   Add errors, add vocab, note what comes back tomorrow.

For a longer `90-120 min` session:

1. foundation block
2. drill block
3. timed mini test
4. correction block

---

## Weekly Rhythm

Suggested baseline:

- `3` sessions focused on `SL`
- `2` sessions focused on `VOC`
- `1-2` sessions with `CE` or `CO`
- `1` timed mixed session when energy is good

The first weeks should bias heavily toward `SL` and `VOC`.

---

## How We Should Operate In This Workspace

When we study here, the default should be:

- source one lesson or one subsection from the foundation books
- convert it into `QCM`, cloze, or short transformations
- connect it to your current error log
- use TCF books to test transfer
- save only the errors and vocabulary that matter

This keeps the work practical and prevents resource overload.
