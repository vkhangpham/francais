## Purpose

Canonical page for the recurring hypothesis error around `si` clauses.

## Canonical rule

In past unreal hypotheses:
- `si` clause -> plus-que-parfait
- main clause -> conditionnel passé

Never place the conditional immediately after `si` in this structure.

## Core model
- `Si j'avais su, j'aurais agi autrement.`
- `Si la ville avait investi plus tôt, elle aurait réduit la pollution automobile.`
- `Si les habitants avaient refusé le projet, la mairie aurait dû le modifier.`

## Typical wrong pattern
- ❌ `Si j'aurais su` -> ✅ `Si j'avais su`

## Why this matters in TCF

This contrast appears naturally in QCM because distractors often place:
- a conditional after `si`
- a present or passé composé where a past hypothetical is needed
- an imperfect where a completed unreal past event is implied

## Safe recognition heuristic
- if the sentence means `if X had happened, Y would have happened`, think:
  - past unreal condition
  - `si + plus-que-parfait`
  - `conditionnel passé` in the main clause
