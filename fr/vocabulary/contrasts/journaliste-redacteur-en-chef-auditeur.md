## Purpose

Canonical contrast page for one of the most persistent media-related confusions in the current TCF cycle.

## Core contrast
- `journaliste` = the person who investigates, writes, verifies, and reports information
- `rédacteur en chef` = the person who directs the editorial team and validates subjects, titles, or hierarchy of information
- `auditeur` = the person who listens to the radio, a lecture, or another spoken program

## Why the confusion matters

In QCM, all three words belong to the same broad media universe.
The trap works because only one of them has editorial authority.

## Stable distinction
- `journaliste` produces or reports content
- `rédacteur en chef` supervises and validates editorial work
- `auditeur` receives content by listening

## Source-backed examples
- `La journaliste a vérifié ses sources avant de publier.`
- `Le rédacteur en chef a refusé ce titre trop sensationnaliste.`
- `Un auditeur écoute la radio ou un cours.`

## Quick TCF heuristic
If the person validates subjects, titles, or editorial decisions, the safest answer is usually `rédacteur en chef`, not `journaliste` and never `auditeur`.
