## High-value lexical clusters

### 1. Pollution and harm
- `pollution` = degradation of the environment
- `polluant` = produces pollution
- `nuisible` = harmful; broader than `polluant`
- useful contrast:
  - a polluting car = `polluante`
  - a polluted city = `polluée`
  - something can be `nuisible` without being pollution in the strict sense

### 2. Transport and fuel
- `gaz d'échappement` = gases emitted by vehicles
- `carburant` = fuel or fuel type depending on context
- `essence sans plomb` = unleaded fuel; less toxic than old leaded fuel, but still fossil fuel
- key clarification:
  - `du carburant` = fuel as substance
  - `un carburant` = a fuel type

### 3. Waste and recycling
- `faire le tri` = sort waste
- `conteneur` = large container for collecting waste
- `verre usagé` = used glass, not necessarily broken
- `incinération` = destruction of waste by burning
- `usine de traitement` = treatment facility

### 4. Urban planning and protection
- `aménager` = adapt or organize a place for a practical purpose
- `piste cyclable` = bike lane
- `espace vert` = green space
- `parc naturel` = protected natural space

### 5. Risks and disasters
- `crue` = rising water level of a river or stream
- `inondation` = flood event produced by overflowing water
- key contrast:
  - `crue` is the rise of the water
  - `inondation` is the flooding consequence
- related disaster vocabulary also reviewed: `raz de marée`, `littoral`, `décombres`
